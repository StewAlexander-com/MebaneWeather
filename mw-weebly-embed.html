◆ Consensus Formula & Methodology
Consensus PoP = (NWS × 0.40) + (GFS × 0.20) + (ECMWF × 0.25) + (HRRR × 0.15)
NWS weighted highest: official forecast with local expertise
ECMWF second: best global skill score for 24-48h precipitation
GFS third: NCEP primary model, direct model guidance
HRRR fourth: high-res but shorter range, best for convective timing
ICON included for spread awareness (unweighted in consensus)
Radar reflectivity threat = estimated from CAPE + thunder PoP + consensus PoP
Threat classification: TSTM (thunder>20% & PoP≥25%), RAIN (≥50%), SHOWER (≥25%), SLIGHT (≥12%), LOW (≥5%), CLEAR

What you’re seeing
🌞
Hour cards
Each card is one hour of the day, midnight to midnight. Tap or drag the slider to explore any hour. Past hours are dimmed — you can’t un-rain the morning.
🔴
NOW marker
The orange ring & needle show right now. Everything left of it already happened. Everything right of it is a forecast — an educated guess about the future.
📊
Today at a Glance
The purple-bordered strip above the slider shows the day’s best and worst in one shot — peak temp, dewpoint, wind, rain chance, rainfall, and storm risk. Tap any value to jump straight to that hour. Tap hi or lo on temp & dewpoint separately.
🧬
Precip % (PoP)
Probability of Precipitation — if 100 days looked exactly like today, rain would fall on this many of them. 50% is genuinely a coin flip.
💧
QPF (rainfall)
Quantitative Precipitation Forecast — the actual amount of water the models expect to fall, in inches. 0.01″ is a sprinkle. 1.00″ is a soaker.
The five weather computers
◆
NWS / NAM
The US government’s official forecast. A meteorologist in Raleigh looked at all the models and drew their best line. Weighted 40% in the consensus — most trusted for local detail.
◆
GFS
Global Forecast System — the US’s main planet-wide computer model. It runs the whole atmosphere as math, 4× a day. Weighted 20%. Good at large patterns; less sharp on exact timing.
◆
ECMWF
Europe’s model, widely considered the world’s most accurate for 1–5 day forecasts. Weighted 25%. When ECMWF and GFS disagree, pay attention.
◆
HRRR
High-Resolution Rapid Refresh — a tight 3km zoom on the US, updated every hour. Best at knowing where and when a storm will hit in the next few hours. Weighted 15%.
◆
ICON
Germany’s model (Deutscher Wetterdienst). Shown for spread awareness — if ICON looks very different from the others, it’s a signal the atmosphere is being unpredictable. Not in the consensus math.
⚖
Consensus
A weighted average of NWS, GFS, ECMWF & HRRR. Think of it as asking four experts and listening more to the ones with the best track record. NWS×0.40 + ECMWF×0.25 + GFS×0.20 + HRRR×0.15.
Storm energy indicators
⚡
CAPE (J/kg)
Convective Available Potential Energy — the fuel in the air for thunderstorms. Imagine a bubble of warm air: CAPE measures how hard it wants to rocket upward. <300 weak · 300–1000 moderate · >1000 explosive.
🠕
Lifted Index (LI)
Lift a parcel of air to 18,000 ft — if it’s warmer than the surrounding air, it keeps rising (unstable). Positive = stable, nothing happens. Negative = unstable. −4 or below = severe risk.
🏆
Thunder %
The official NWS probability that thunder will be heard during this hour at your location. Even 10% means you should keep an eye on the sky. At 30%+ it’s a real possibility.
Radar & threat colours
📡
Radar dBZ
Radar shouts at rain droplets and listens for the echo. The louder the echo (higher dBZ), the bigger the drops and the heavier the rain. This is estimated from model data, not a live radar scan.
🌊
dBZ scale
<15 — Dry / cloud only 15–25 — Light drizzle 25–35 — Light rain 35–45 — Moderate rain 45–55 — Heavy / hail possible >55 — Severe / large hail
⚠
Threat badges
TSTM — Thunder likely, stay aware RAIN — Steady rain expected (≥50%) SHOWER — Passing showers (≥25%) SLIGHT — Small chance (≥12%) LOW — Unlikely but possible (≥5%) CLEAR — No meaningful rain signal
Severe weather risk (SPC)
🌧
SPC Day 1 Risk
NONE — Normal day, no severe risk MRGL — Marginal: one or two isolated storms SLGT — Slight: scattered stronger storms ENH — Enhanced: widespread, stay informed MDT — Moderate: significant event, take action HIGH — High: major outbreak, seek shelter
